Time-Dependent Vector Field

Definition. A time-dependent vector field is just a function of signature $\Real^{d+1} \rightarrow \Real$. We typically view it as a function $v$ of time $t \in \Real$ and position $x \in \Real^d$. The output $v(t,x)$ or $v_t(x)$ is a position in $\Real^d$.

We often view $v_t$ when $t$ is fixed as a vector field on $\Real^d$.

We visualize a time-dependent vector field with a video of vector fields. You will see arrows dancing.

Here's a time-dependent vector field. \begin{align*} v_t(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos t \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \end{align*} It's not a function of $x$, but it is surely a function of $t$. Here's what it looks like when visualized.
