Approaches for General Generative Modeling

We have only managed to solve generative modeling in the simplest case: Gaussian to another Gaussian.

What do people do when we want to solve the general problem?

$\xrightarrow{\ \ \ f \ \ \ }$
$p_{\mrm{noise}}$ $p_{\mrm{data}}$

Well, we try to model the function $f$ with a neural network $f_\theta$.

One-Step Transformation

$f_\theta$ transforms the noise item $x_{\mrm{noise}} \sim p_{\mrm{noise}}$ into a data item $x_{\data}$ in one step. $$x_{\data} = f_\theta(x_{\mrm{noise}}).$$

VAEs and GANs uses this approach.

Actually, for this approach, the size of $x_{\mrm{noise}}$ does not have to match that of $x_{\mrm{data}}$.

Typically, $x_{\mrm{noise}} \in \Real^d$ and $x_{\mrm{\data}} \in \Real^D$ where $d \ll D$. So, $f$ is not a diffeomorphism per se.

This approach has difficulties.

  • VAEs have issues with quality of genarated $x_{\mrm{data}}$.
  • GANs can generate high-quality $x_{\mrm{data}}$ but are very difficult to train.
