(Uniform) Scaling

Definition. A (uniform) scaling is a vector function $f: \Real^d \ra \Real^d$ of the form $$f(x) = \sigma x$$ where $\sigma \in \Real$.

Example 1. f(x) = 2x

$\xrightarrow{\ \ \ f\ \ \ }$

The image becomes 2 times larger

Example 2. $f(x) = -x$

$\xrightarrow{\ \ \ f\ \ \ }$

The image is flipped both horizontally and vertically.

We shall denote scaling by a scalar $c$ in two ways.

  • $\texttt{S}_\sigma$
  • $\sigma\, \mrm{id}$

So, $$\texttt{S}_{\sigma}(x) = (\sigma\, \mrm{id})(x) = \sigma x.$$
