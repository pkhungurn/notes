Flow Matching

The flow matching paper [Lipman et al. 2022] proposes a way to train CNF without simulation.

If we know the correct time-dependent vector field $v_t(x)$ is before hand, we can train a neural network $v(t,x;\theta)$ to approximates the correct vector field by minimizing the following flow matching loss: \begin{align*} \mcal{L}_{\mrm{FM}}(\theta) = E_{\substack{t \sim \mcal{U}([0,1]), \\ x \sim p_t }} \big[ \| v_t(x) - v(t, x; \theta) \|^2 \big]. \end{align*} After training finishes, we can use $v(t,x;\theta)$ as a drop-in replacement for $v_t(x)$.

The problem is that we don't know what $v_t(x)$ is.

The paper does two things.

  • It suggests a way to construct $v$ from individual samples of $p_{\data}$.
  • It introduces another loss, the conditional flow matching loss, that is simpler to evaluate than the flow matching loss, but has the exact same gradient. So, we can use the conditional flow matching loss during training, and we will get the same vector field (theoretically).
