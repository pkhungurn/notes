Generating Probability Paths with Flows

In generative modeling, we typically do not care about the exact form of probability distribution.
We may just want to sample from $p_{\data}$.

In case of a probability path $p_t$, we do not care about the exact form of $p_t$.
We just want to sample from it.

This is just like a transformation of probability distribution by a push-forward $q = [f]_* p$

  • We know how to sample from $p$.
  • We know how to apply $f$.
  • Notice that we don't know anything about $q$, but we can sample from it by just knowing the two items above.

There's an analogue of the push-foward for probability flow. However, it requires one new concept.

Definition. A flow $\phi$ in an indexed famility of vector functions $\{ \phi_t : t \in \Real \}$ that satisfies the following property.

  • Each $\phi_t$ has signature $\Real^d \ra \Real^d$.
  • $\phi_0 = \mrm{id}$.
  • $\phi_t$ is a diffeomorphism.
  • When $\phi$ is viewed as function $\phi(t,x)$ of signature $\Real^{d+1} \ra \Real^d$ is differentiable.

Definition. A push-forward of probability distribution $p$ according to a flow $\phi_t$ is probability path $p_t$ defined by $p_t = [\phi_t]_* p$.

So, to sample from $p_t$ which is the push-forward of $p$ according to $\phi_t$, we do the following.

  1. Sample $x \sim p$.
  2. Compute $x_t = \phi_t(x)$.
  3. Return $x_t$ to the user.
