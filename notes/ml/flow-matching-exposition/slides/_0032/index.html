Probability Distribution

We saw that a vector function can transform images.

A probability distribution $p: \Real^d \ra \Real^{+} \cup \{0 \}$ (or a probability density function) is a special kind of image. It has the property that \begin{align*} \int p(x)\, \dee x = 1. \end{align*} Here, the integration domain is the whole $\Real^d$.

Question. What does $p(x)$ mean?

Answer. Let's say $x = (x^1, x^2, \dotsc, x^d)$. It means that, in the infinitesimally small volume $$[x^1, x^1+ \dee x^1) \times [x^2, x+ \dee x^2) \times \dotsb \times [x^d, x+ \dee x^d)$$ that contains $x$, there exists "probability mass" in this volume, and the amount of this probability mass is \begin{align*} p(x)\, \dee x = p(x)\, \dee x^1 \dee x^2 \dotsm \dee x^d. \end{align*} Here, each $\dee x^i$ is an infinitesimally small quantity such that $(\dee x^i)^2 = 0$.

(This is hand-wavy physics math. Use with caution.)
