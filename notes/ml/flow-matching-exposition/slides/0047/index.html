Examples of Flows.

Example 1. The function $$\phi_t(x) = x + (t,0)$$ is a flow.

  • $\phi_0(x) = x + (0,0) = x$.
  • The derivative is given by \begin{align*} \nabla \phi_t(x) = \nabla \begin{bmatrix} x^1 + t \\ x^2 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial (x^1 + t)}{\partial t} & \frac{\partial (x^1 + t)}{\partial x^1} & \frac{\partial (x^1 + t)}{\partial x^2} \\ \frac{\partial x^2}{\partial t} & \frac{\partial x^2}{\partial x^1} & \frac{\partial x^2}{\partial x^2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}. \end{align*} So, $\phi$ is a differentiable function.
  • $\phi_t$ as a function of signature $\Real^d \ra \Real^d$ is the translation $\texttt{T}_{(t,0)}$. So it is a diffeomorphism.

Example 2. The function $$\phi_t(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos t & -\sin t \\ \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix} x$$ is a flow.

  • $\phi_0(x) = Ix = x$.
  • The derivative is given by \begin{align*} \nabla \phi_t(x) &= \begin{bmatrix} \nabla_1 \phi_t(x) & \nabla_{\S 2} \phi_t(x) \end{bmatrix} \\ \nabla_1 \phi_t(x) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \bigg( \begin{bmatrix} \cos t & -\sin t \\ \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix} x \bigg) = \begin{bmatrix} -\sin t & -\cos t \\ \cos t & -\sin t \end{bmatrix} x \\ \nabla_{\S 2} \phi_t(x) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \bigg( \begin{bmatrix} \cos t & -\sin t \\ \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix} x \bigg) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos t & -\sin t \\ \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix}. \end{align*} All entries are continuous funtions of $t$ and $x$. So, $\phi$ is differentiable.
  • $\phi_t$ as a function of signature $\Real^d \ra \Real^d$ is just a rotation of $x$ around the origin by $t$ radians. So it is a diffeomorphism.
