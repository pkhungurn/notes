Continuous Normalizing Flow?

The main idea of normalzing flow is interesting.

It says that, to transform $p_{\mrm{noise}}$ into $p_{\mrm{data}}$, we subject $p_{\mrm{noise}}$ to a series of vector functions: $$f_{\theta_1}, f_{\theta_2}, f_{\theta_3}, \dotsc, f_{\theta_{K-1}}, f_{\theta_K}.$$ This gives us as series of push-forwards $$p_{\mrm{noise}}, [f_{\theta_1}]_* p_{\mrm{noise}}, [f_{\theta_2 \dotsm \theta_1}]_* p_{\mrm{noise}}, [f_{\theta_3 \dotsm \theta_1}]_* p_{\mrm{noise}}, \dotsc, [f_{\theta_K \dotsm \theta_1}]_* p_{\mrm{noise}} $$ The hope is that the last distribution $[f_{\theta_K \dotsm \theta_1}]_* p_{\mrm{noise}}$ is a good approximation of $p_{\data}$.

In other words, $p_{\mrm{noise}}$ is gradually transformed into $p_{\data}$ in multiple steps.

What if the transformation is not in discrete steps but happens continuously?
Like, can $p_{\mrm{noise}}$ evolves continuously over time to $p_{\data}$?
