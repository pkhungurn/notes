Choosing $\mu_t$ and $\sigma_t$

We know that we can construct the full vector field $v_t(x)$ by integrating the conditional vector field \begin{align*} v_t(x|x_{\data}) = \frac{\sigma_t'(x_{\data})}{\sigma_t(x_{\data})} (x - \mu_t(x_{\data})) + \mu'_t(x_{\data}). \end{align*}

However, we haven't actually picked $\mu_t$ and $\sigma_t$. We just know that any differentiable functions that satisfy the following constraints \begin{align*} \mu_0(x_{\data}) &= 0, \\ \mu_1(x_{\data}) &= x_{\data}, \\ \sigma_0(x_{\data}) &= 1, \\ \sigma_1(x_{\data}) &= \sigma_\min \end{align*} would work.

So, let's pick something concrete. Shall we.
