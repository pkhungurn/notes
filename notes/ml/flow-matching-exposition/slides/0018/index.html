Notation for Derivatives

There are many notations for derivatives: $Df(x)$, $\partial f/\partial x$, $J_f(x)$, and so on.

I'm going to use an unconventional one: $\nabla f(x)$.

If $f$ is a vector function of signature $\Real^n \rightarrow \Real^m$,
then $\nabla f$, the derivative of $f$, is a function of signature $f: \Real^n \ra \Real^{m \times n}$.

If $f$ is a vector function of signature $\Real^n \rightarrow \Real$ (a scalar field),
then $\nabla f$, the derivative of $f$, is a function of signature $f: \Real^n \ra \Real^{1 \times n}$.
We can interpret a matrix of size $\Real^{1 \times n}$ as a vector, and this is exactly the gradient of $f$.
So, I think the notation $\nabla f$ is justified because it agrees with the notation for gradients.

If \begin{align*} f(x) = \begin{bmatrix} f^1(x) \\ f^2(x) \\ \vdots \\ f^m(x) \end{bmatrix} \end{align*} where each $f^i$ is of signature $\Real^n \ra \Real$, then \begin{align*} \nabla f(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f^1}{\partial x^1}(x) & \frac{\partial f^1}{\partial x^2}(x) & \cdots & \frac{\partial f^1}{\partial x^n}(x) \\ \frac{\partial f^2}{\partial x^1}(x) & \frac{\partial f^2}{\partial x^2}(x) & \cdots & \frac{\partial f^2}{\partial x^n}(x) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f^m}{\partial x^1}(x) & \frac{\partial f^m}{\partial x^2}(x) & \cdots & \frac{\partial f^m}{\partial x^n}(x) \end{bmatrix}. \end{align*}

I will sometimes denote partial derivatives also with the $\nabla$ notation. \begin{align*} \nabla_{j} f^i = \frac{\partial f^i}{\partial x^j}. \end{align*} This is quite nice because the position of $j$ and $i$ indicates which one should go to the top and which one should go to the bottom.

In other words, we can write \begin{align*} \nabla f(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \nabla_1f^1(x) & \nabla_2f^1(x) & \cdots & \nabla_nf^1(x) \\ \nabla_1f^2(x) & \nabla_2f^2(x) & \cdots & \nabla_nf^2(x) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \nabla_1f^m(x) & \nabla_2f^m(x) & \cdots & \nabla_nf^m(x) \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{align*}
